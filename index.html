Storyboard Collection

Storyboard & Visual Planning Collection

Storyboard Artist / Story Planner / Visual Director

A collection of storyboard studies exploring the early stages of animation pre-production, focusing on visual storytelling and production planning.

PROJECT OVERVIEW
Storyboard & Visual Planning Collection is a collection of storyboard works created to explore the early stages of animation production. This project focuses on transforming written ideas and story concepts into visual sequences, shot compositions, camera directions, scene timing, and emotional flow. Rather than presenting a single finished film, this collection highlights my understanding of the pre-production process, where the foundation of an animated work is planned before the final production begins. Through these storyboard studies, I focused on how to guide the viewer's attention, control pacing, build atmosphere, and design scenes that are clear, engaging, and emotionally effective.

MY ROLE & CONTRIBUTIONS
I worked on story planning, scene breakdown, storyboard creation, shot composition, camera direction, timing notes, and visual flow. My process included analyzing the story, deciding which moments should be emphasized, planning how each shot should be framed, and considering how the viewer would experience the scene from beginning to end. I explored how camera angles, character placement, movement, rhythm, and scene transitions can affect the audience's understanding and emotional response.

A major focus was understanding the viewer's experience, considering: What does the audience need to understand in this shot? Where should the viewer's eye go first? How should the camera angle affect the emotion of the scene? How can the scene be made clearer and more enjoyable?

Study 1: Film Introduction

Written Idea:
Film introduction and opening sequence as storyboard

Visual Planning:
Establishing camera angles and framing, converting to anime style with color planning (static boards, not animation yet)

Study 2: Animation Pipeline

Written Idea:
Transition from storyboard to douga (rough animation) and layout stages

Visual Planning:
Demonstrating the anime production pipeline, showing how storyboards translate through layout and animation stages

This collection helped me improve my foundation in animation by focusing on movement itself, allowing me to study the technical side of animation such as how motion should feel, how poses connect, and how timing affects the viewer's perception.
